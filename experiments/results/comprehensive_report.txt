[Query 2] What happens if a student fails a course?
  [LLM Answer] According to Excerpt 1, if a student fails a course, they will be counted as having one F grade, even if they repeat the course and fail again (irrespective of the number of times they repeat). This is stated in point (5) under the subheading "Clearance of W/F/XF Grades" in Excerpt 1.

Additionally, according to Excerpt 1, if a student fails a course and repeats it, their transcript will show both the old grade and the new earned grade, but the better grade will be used in the computation of CGPA. This is stated in point (3) under the subheading "Clearance of W/F/XF Grades" in Excerpt 1.

If a student fails a course and it is a pre-requisite for a course in the following semester, they will have to clear it through retest within the first six academic weeks of the next semester. This is stated in Excerpt 3, under the subheading "Retest of End Semester Examination".

[Query 3] What is the attendance policy?
  [LLM Answer] According to Excerpt 1, the attendance policy is as follows:

- A student is not allowed to appear in the final examination of a subject in which their attendance is less than 75%, and they will be awarded an XF grade in that subject. (Excerpt 1, Section 4, Subsection a)
- A student who misses any examination/test due to illness or other acceptable reasons approved by the DBS/FBS, provided their attendance in a particular course is not less than 75%, is given I as a grade. (Excerpt 1, Section 4, Subsection b)

Additionally, according to Excerpt 2, a student will be dropped from the MS programme if their attendance remains below 75% in more than two courses, and an XF grade will be awarded in the course in which the student's attendance is below 75%. (Excerpt 2, Section 3, Subsection e)

[Query 4] How many times can a course be repeated?

[Query 6] How are final grades calculated?
  [LLM Answer] According to Excerpt 1, the final grade is calculated as follows:

"For subjects having two categories (theory and practical) combined, the final grade is computed by multiplying the number of credits assigned to each category with the percentage marks obtained in that category and dividing the sum of the two by the total credits in that subject."

For example, as mentioned in Excerpt 1, for the subject "Physics" with 3 theory credits and 1 practical credit, the overall grade is calculated as:

"Overall grade = (70 X 3+90 X 1)/ (3+1) =75 %"

Additionally, Excerpt 1 states that the overall subject percentage is rounded off to two decimal points by the individual faculty.

The overall grading scheme is also mentioned in Excerpt 2, which lists the numerical grade and grade point for each letter grade.

[Query 14] What are the rules for exam re-checking?
  [LLM Answer] According to Excerpt 2 (Annex B to Para 22 to Chapter 2) and Excerpt 3 (Annex A to Para 30 to Chapter 2), the rules for exam re-checking are as follows:

1. Fill out the re-checking application form completely.
2. Deposit re-checking fee amounting to Rs 500/- per paper and enclose original deposit slip along with the application form.
3. Clearly mention the subject/paper required to be re-checked.
4. An application form shall be entertained only if it is complete in all respects and is received in the office along with the prescribed fee within 15 days after the declaration of the relevant result.
5. Incomplete and incorrect forms shall be returned to the candidate for completion and if it is not submitted within the prescribed time limit, no action shall be taken on them.
6. Time-barred applications shall not be entertained.
7. Re-checking does not mean re-assessment or re-evaluation of the answer-book.

Additionally, the following conditions are ensured during the re-checking process:
- There is no mistake in the grand total on the title page of the answer book.
- The total of various parts of a question has been correctly made at the end of each question.
- All totals have been correctly brought forward on the title page of the answer book.
- No portion of the answer book has been left un-marked.
- The total number of questions attempted does not exceed the limit allowed in the Question Paper.
- Total marks in the answer-book tally with the marks sheet.
- Viva/practical and internal assessment marks are not re-tabulated.
- The student or anybody on their behalf has no right to see or examine the answer-book for any purpose.
- An increase/decrease in the final marks of the student will be decided according to the results of re-checking.
